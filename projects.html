Enhancing urban mobility for differently abled individuals

Role

UX/UI Designer

Duration

2.5 Weeks

Tools

Figma, FigJam, Slack, Discord

The differently-abled population has a unique challenge of transitioning from inaccessible urban environments to navigating public transportation systems designed without their specific needs in mind.

For the first time, they take full control over their navigation and transportation, choosing how, where, and when to travel. The project started from my frustration when witnessing the daily struggles of people with disabilities navigating an inaccessible urban environment.

The Challenge

How can we help differently-abled individuals in Vancouver navigate urban environments and public transportation systems?

  • 1. Make urban environments easier to navigate for accessible decisions.
  • 2. Create a user-friendly application to track the user's needs.
  • 3. Make the app customizable to meet individual accessibility needs and preferences.

The Solution

A comprehensive Smart Pathway App that enhances urban mobility for individuals with disabilities by providing personalized route options, effectively addressing challenges like inaccessible public transport and lack of accessible pathways.

Personalized experience

Customized route plans to enhance urban mobility for individuals with disabilities, addressing their unique accessibility needs and preferences for a smoother, more accessible journey.

Connect with a caregiver

Easily connect with your caregiver or find a new one through our caregiver feature. Over 50% of people with disabilities rely on caregivers. This helps coordinate travel plans, ensuring seamless and supported journeys.

Community contributions

User contributions play a vital role. By sharing experiences and reporting obstacles, users help improve the app, making navigation more accurate and helpful for everyone.

Research

Understanding the problem

Before beginning the project, I used social media platforms like Reddit to sample insights into the experiences and perspectives of people with disabilities regarding public transportation.

My research led me to a few emerging patterns that guided our direction:

It depends on how accessible the transit system is, but for the most part transit operators try to be as accessible as possible. However, the most difficult part is getting to and from transit stops to my home.

The primary challenge is in the first and the last mile of the journey. Based on each user's needs, this could mean lack of places to rest, limited curb cuts, steep inclines, poor lighting, etc.

Speaking as someone who is special needs and an advocate for the special needs community, it's really challenging on public transport, both for the caretaker and the special needs person themselves.

Public transport poses challenges for both special needs individuals and their caretakers. As such, this told us there needs to be a “Caretaker Support” feature that caters to both users.

Competitive Analysis

After better understanding our users, I listed down the features of leading navigation apps from the App Store and noted the differentiations and overlaps through a Venn Diagram. I was particularly interested in the overlaps in features because it gave me a good baseline of the features we should consider for Urban Mobility App.

From this I concluded...

Inaccessibility and lack of clear information are preventing differently-abled individuals from effectively using public transport.

This begged the questions of:

How can we improve the accessibility and convenience to ensure a satisfying travel experience?
How can we develop features that provide clear information and support for both special needs users and their caretakers?

Kicking Off

Designing an accessible and intuitive experience

After analyzing the features of competitor navigation apps and familiarizing with problems our users face in urban mobility, we did Crazy 8's exercise focusing on brainstorming new ideas.

Each team member sketched their ideas, pitched them, and we voted on our favorites.

We selected the most promising concepts, mindful of our time limits and began creating the app.

Wireframes

After more brainstorming sessions, we refined our concepts and selected the best ideas for further development.

Wireframes

These sketches were then transformed into wireframes, which later led to the final prototypes.

Style guide

Crafting an inclusive indentity

Creating an experience that conveys a sense of reliability, clarity, and inclusivity. The colours reflect the balance between professional functionality and approachable design, making the experience both effective and welcoming for users. The copy language is considerate and straightforward.

Conclussion

Learnings and future plans

The long process of researching, testing, and refining this design has led to a project I’m happy with, but I know there's still a lot of room for improvement. I'm most curious to see how new findings within the space I want to explore will continue to evolve my design.

What I learned

Get help: Asking for feedback is invaluable. Fresh perspectives helped me catch details I missed.

Word choices matter: Choosing the right words can be even harder than design decisions, but it's crucial for clarity and inclusivity.

Areas for improvement

Feature Development: Fully develop the features of every page and enhance interactions.

Enhance “Connect with a Caregiver”: Do further user testing to determine whether it’s a feature worth developing.

User Testing: Conduct tests with target users to identify what other challenges they face and create a sample curriculum to challenge the effectiveness of our methods.
